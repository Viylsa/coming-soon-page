## Executive summary

VIYLSA's landing page is genuinely well-built — disciplined design tokens, a real embedded client tour, accessible FAQ/details, prefers-reduced-motion handling, and a coherent crimson-on-warm-ink palette. It is not slop; it is a tasteful template that hasn't yet earned its "premium B2B" claim. The four things holding it back are concrete and fixable: (1) a confirmed first-paint bug where the hero marquee renders below the fold, compounded by a worse, unflagged-until-now hydration flash where the whole prerendered page blinks to opacity:0 on every real visit; (2) uniform fade-up motion applied identically to every element, which is exactly the "basic animations" the founder names; (3) an AI-guide that is over-weighted in nav, hero copy, a full section, and the footer, against the founder's explicit wish to demote it; and (4) a structural sameness — every section opens with eyebrow + serif-italic headline, three light "haze" sections cluster with no tonal cadence, and trust proof (the real NUTECH tour) sits behind an abstract problem section with no client name, testimonial, or founder photos. There are also real reliability and credibility risks: the WhatsApp conversion depends on window.open (fails silently in in-app browsers and shows false success), and both the analytics dashboard and the AI demo assert fabricated-precision figures (342 visits, "↑24% vs last week", Rs 45,000 fees, 30 August deadline) for a one-client startup. The plan below fixes the breakage first, demotes the AI guide and re-sequences the page for proof-first credibility, then elevates motion into 3 signature moments rather than more motion everywhere, then does the content/visual polish that adds up to a premium feel. Ordered so the site is never broken mid-way and the cheapest credibility wins land first.

## Design north star — the "feel" to aim for

"Editorial proof, quietly engineered." VIYLSA is an unknown early-stage studio whose single greatest asset is a real, walkable tour — so the entire site should feel like a confident editorial document that leads with evidence, not claims, and earns "premium" through restraint + a few perfectly-crafted moments rather than constant motion. Three pillars: (1) PROOF FIRST — the real NUTECH tour, real checkable numbers, named client/testimonial and real founder faces carry credibility; mock/illustrative material is clearly labelled so nothing reads as faked traction. (2) RATIONED SIGNATURE MOTION — calm body, with exactly three bespoke, scroll-aware moments (hero headline "locking in", the live-tour player "powering on" via clip wipe, the analytics dashboard "coming alive" as a sequenced KPI→bars→insight story). Everything else is a quiet, well-eased reveal; ambient infinite loops are killed except one genuine "live" signal. (3) COMPOSED TONAL RHYTHM — a deliberate dark/light/dark cadence (hero dark → proof → one dark product frame → … → contact dark) and varied section-head structures (index numerals, label-left layouts, plain Geist heads) so the serif-italic accent feels special because it appears 2-3 times, not on every heading. The tour's deep wine #7F141A is the spine; the bright #DC143C is reserved as one consistent "live/active" signal (scrollspy indicator, count-up numerals). The net feel: a credible institution-grade studio that happens to have impeccable taste — never a Framer/AOS default.

## Marquee-below-the-fold bug — root cause & fix

ROOT CAUSE (confirmed in source, two stacked mechanisms). Geometry: .v-hero (styles.css:319-330) is `display:flex; flex-direction:column; justify-content:center; min-height:clamp(600px,92vh,860px); padding:clamp(120px,16vw,150px) 0 0`. The first child .v-hero__inner has flex:1 and vertically centers the copy; the marquee (Hero.jsx:53-61) is the LAST flex child with `margin-top:clamp(48px,7vw,72px)` + `padding:20px 0` + 1px border (~129px of its own stacked height). On a 1366×768 laptop (~657px usable after chrome) the centered copy block + ~150px top padding push the marquee's top edge to roughly y≈700-740px — below the fold — so it only appears once the user scrolls. Units: the height uses `vh`, not `svh`/`dvh` (no dvh anywhere — grep confirms only `92vh` at line 325), so on mobile Safari/Chrome 92vh resolves against the LARGER toolbar-collapsed viewport while the toolbar is actually showing, computing a hero taller than the visible area and parking the marquee off-screen until a scroll collapses the toolbar (this also causes a visible reflow "pop"). The marquee has no data-reveal, so it is purely geometric, not an observer timing issue. (Secondary, separately confirmed: the marquee is only duplicated 2× in Hero.jsx:55 and the keyframe ends at translateX(-50%), so on ≥1440px monitors a blank gap appears in the loop — a second defect on the same element.)
THE FIX (three coordinated changes): (1) Decouple the marquee from the centered column — give .v-hero `position:relative` and pin the marquee with `position:absolute; left:0; right:0; bottom:0` (it is aria-hidden decoration, so absolute is safe), then add `padding-bottom` on .v-hero equal to the marquee band height so the centered copy never collides with it. This glues the marquee to the visible hero bottom at every height. (2) Switch the unit to small-viewport with a fallback: `min-height:clamp(600px,92vh,860px);` then a second line `min-height:clamp(560px,86svh,820px);` so toolbars are accounted for. (3) Reduce top padding to `clamp(96px,12vw,124px)` (the nav pill sits at top ~22px, height ~52px, so ~88px clears it) and shorten the hero sub-copy so the centered block is smaller. Also fix the seam: render the venue list 4× and end the keyframe at translateX(-25%). Verify the marquee top edge is well above the fold at 1366×768, 1280×720, iPhone (390×664 visible with toolbar), and iPad 1024 landscape.
